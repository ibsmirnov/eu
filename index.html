Research Highlights

more_vert Schools are segregated in the digital space

Read the paper

close

There are many studies that explore friendhship links withing educational institutions, e.g. within one school or one university. Digital traces allow studying social ties on a new level. In this paper, I investigate interschool online friendship on a scale of the whole city. I find that schools are segregated by academic performance in the digital space. This result is especially interesting because the same was not true for the physical space.

more_vert Predicting academic performance from short texts

Read the paper

close

In this paper, I build a model to predict academic performance from short texts on social media. I combined unsupervised learning of word embeddings on a large corpus of social media posts with a supervised model trained on data from nationally representative sample. This helped to achieve high transferability of the model. Thanks to the word embeddings, it is possible to compute performance scores for millions of words that are fun to explore!

more_vert Parents mention sons more often than daughters

Read the paper

close

We use data from a Russian social networking site on posts made by more than 600,000 users and find that parents mention sons more often than daughters and that posts featuring sons get more "likes."
